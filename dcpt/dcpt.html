So, What is prototyping?

Prototyping is a method of answering questions.

How do we prototype?

Commonly we expect prototypes to be hi-fi —

— but for all you Slide Deck Jockeys out there, this is OK too.

Today, we're going to prototype with paper.

Determine what you're trying to answer.

Step One:

We believe that [doing this / building this feature / creating this experience]

for [these people / personas],

will achieve [this outcome]

as measured by [feedback / quantitative measure / qualitative insight].

Start with a Hypothesis —

Choose an appropriate medium and fidelity.

Step Two:

The level of fidelity in your work should

be appropriate to the type of feedback

you (and your team) want to receive.

Too Lo-Fi —

It's difficult to communicate what you're talking about; you invite high-level conceptual feedback.

Too Hi-Fi —

People/stakeholders can loose the big picture; people critique the little details, nitpick color choices, etc.

All in all, be comfortable with seeing work

that isn't high-fidelity OR fully-baked;

it's all a natural part of the process.

Iterate (Rapidly)

Step Three:

Prototyping is not a point in the process. It is the process.
